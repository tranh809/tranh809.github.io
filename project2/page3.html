Final Poem

Floating naked with his eyes open,

He had drowned.

Meditated ecstasy in which he lay drifting,

A man no longer of this world.

Dare come closer, in a hushed voice

To awaken him

Before five so they could leave at dawn.

His trance,

In the half-light, the clear blue eyes.

The impassive dignity held in his hand,

Lacked strength.

But he rose up from the waters,

So wasted a body.

"Let's go,"

he said,

"as fast as we can.

No one loves us here."
